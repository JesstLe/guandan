# AI-Use Disclosure Draft

This project used AI assistance during research engineering and manuscript
preparation. The assistance included brainstorming research framing, organizing
related-work notes, drafting and revising project documentation, writing
experiment-pipeline code, generating tests, and producing checklist-style
reviews of submission readiness.

All experimental claims, numerical results, source interpretations, and final
submission decisions remain the authors' responsibility. AI-generated text and
code must be reviewed by the authors before submission. No unpublished
manuscript, private data, or full research corpus should be sent to an external
model provider without explicit author approval and a recorded provenance entry.

For this project specifically, current LLM experiment outputs have not yet been
generated. When real model runs are performed, the submission package must record
the model provider, model name/version, run date, temperature, sampling
parameters, batch file, provider result file, raw-output directory, retries,
exclusions, and any parse-repair policy.

Working venue policy target: AAMAS 2026. The checked AAMAS policy says
AI-assisted technologies may not be listed as authors or co-authors; allows AI
assistance for polishing, formatting, and code/scripts for proof-of-concept,
demonstrations, and experiments; and asks for detailed methods-section
information, including prompt, tool, and version, when AI is used to create
hypotheses or methodologies, including experimental design.
